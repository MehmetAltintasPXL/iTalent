The chatbot had to enable easy communication not only via text but also with emojis and images. My personal objective was to build a chatbot myself for the first time and gain experience with open-source technologies like Streamlit and Ollama.

We clearly divided roles: Kerim and Maarten crafted a clean Angular frontend with buttons for text, emojis, and media; Mirthe prepared a polished PowerPoint presentation; and I focused on backend development and server management. I chose Streamlit to quickly develop a Python-based chatbot capable of understanding questions and emojis, and I ran the service on an AWS EC2 server. To avoid cloud costs, I used Ollama to host the LLaMAÂ 2 model locally, keeping the solution entirely open-source.

To ensure reliable answers, I implemented a Python script that automatically crawled the Red Cross website, extracting essential information and storing it in a JSON file with links to original sources. This Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) approach guaranteed factual responses rather than guesses.

Next, I containerized the entire backend in Docker, enabling straightforward deployment and recovery. Initially, performance was limited by our first server, but AWS engineer Lukas provided a more powerful EC2 instance, greatly improving response times.

At the end of the hackathon, a jury evaluated all entries. Despite arriving immediately after exams and working without external support, we did not win a prize but were proud of our open-source, fully functional chatbot and our ability to learn and collaborate effectively under tight deadlines.

Through this experience, I learned the importance of clear task division, rapid prototyping, and requesting help when needed. My strengths were perseverance and adaptability; I gained confidence in using Streamlit, Docker, and AI models. For future projects, I aim to allocate more time for thorough testing. This hackathon aligns well with my studies in AWS, Docker, Python, and AI, and demonstrates my capacity to deliver impactful solutions under pressure.
